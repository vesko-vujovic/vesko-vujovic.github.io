# Introduction

In this blog post, we will tackle a very interesting topic, that is in my opinion very overlooked. We use the concept of time as a reference for our actions on a daily basis. I can bet that many of us didn't think about the fact of how reliable our time measurement is i.e. what implications it has on our systems if it's not accurate.


# A little bit of history

Since ancient times human race has tried to measure time based on some natural events. We have a lot of evidence for that. There are a lot of interesting devices that were used to achieve this. One of them was an Egyptian device that dates back to c. 1500 BC when they used a sundial to know how much time is it. 

Do you wonder sometimes why they used the number **12** for total hours throughout the day? The importance of the number 12 is due to the number of **lunar cycles** in a year and the number of stars used to count the passage of night. There are 12,3601 lunar cycles per year or that's how many cycles it takes for Earth to make a full circle around the sun. 

After many iterations and clock evolutions throughout the life of the human race, we started to measure our time based on the fact of how much it takes for Earth to make a full rotation around its axis and we all know that is `almost` 24 hours. I emphasized on purpose the word **almost** later on we will see why.
